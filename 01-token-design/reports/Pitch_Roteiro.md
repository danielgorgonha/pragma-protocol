# Pitch script: Pragma Protocol (PGM)

Methodology: NEPQ (Neuro-Emotional Persuasive Questions).  
Duration: 5 minutes. Format: Video.

## Principles

- Start with the problem the audience recognises, not with "I will present X".
- Use rhetorical questions so the audience reaches the conclusion themselves.
- Neutral, curious tone. Problem, then consequence, then solution.
- Mark pauses in the script as `[pause]`.

---

## Slide 1 — Cover (~25s)

Calm, direct tone. Look at the camera.

*"Before talking about the token itself, I want to ask a question.*

*How many Web3 projects have you seen start with an interesting proposition — and die quietly because the economics didn't sustain the ecosystem?*

`[pause — 2s]`

*That's the problem I wanted to understand. That's where the Pragma Protocol came from."*

---

## Slide 2 — The problem (~55s)

Calm tone. Speak slowly.

*"Think with me.*

*In the Web3 services market — development, audits, consulting — transactions happen in ETH or stablecoins. The provider delivers, gets paid, and leaves. The buyer pays and leaves.*

*What did the platform get?*

`[pause]`

*Nothing.*

*If no value stays in the ecosystem, what is the real incentive to stay? To trust? To return?*

`[pause]`

*No retention incentive. No value capture. No alignment between seller and buyer. That's not an execution problem. It's a design problem."*

---

## Slide 3 — The solution (~65s)

Slightly firmer, still neutral.

*"The question that guided the design of PGM was simple: how do you create a token that gives people a reason to stay — not just use and leave?*

`[pause]`

*Two parts.*

*First: if every marketplace transaction is settled in PGM and the platform keeps 2% of each operation, that value doesn't disappear. It feeds the ecosystem reserve.*

*Second: if holding 500 PGM cuts your fee from 2% to 1%, gets you featured and priority with new clients, you have a concrete reason not to sell.*

`[pause]`

*That lowers V in MV=PQ — circulation velocity. Less selling pressure. More stability. It's mechanics, not enthusiasm."*

---

## Slide 4 — Tokenomics (~50s)

Technical, objective. No superlatives.

*"Supply is fixed: 10 million PGM. No mint after deploy.*

*Why fixed? Because any emission mechanism controlled by governance in an early stage creates a manipulation vector. Scarcity can't depend on goodwill.*

*Distribution: 40% public sale, no vesting. 30% ecosystem, released by usage. 20% team — 12-month cliff, 24 months linear. No one sells at launch. 10% strategic reserve, 6-month cliff.*

*Simple. No magic."*

---

## Slide 5 — Sustainability (~55s)

Serious, not dramatic.

*"Axie Infinity had a model that looked sustainable. In under two years the economy imploded. Why? Emission had no brake. No reason to hold — so everyone sold.*

`[pause]`

*PGM's design avoids those mistakes. Fixed supply, 2% fee for flow, premium access for velocity control, vesting for launch protection. In Lesson 02 we add a BME contract: adaptive burn inspired by EIP-1559. Under high demand, supply contracts. The token can become deflationary. Theory and code connected."*

---

## Slide 6 — Closing (~30s)

Calm. Slower. Look at the camera.

*"The question any token must answer: why would someone hold this token instead of selling it tomorrow?*

`[pause — 3s]`

*Pragma Protocol has a concrete answer. Fixed supply. Real value capture. Structural retention incentive. Team alignment. The repo is on GitHub — one-pager, BME contract, tests.*

*Thank you."*

## Tone guide

| Phase       | Aim           | Avoid                |
|-------------|---------------|----------------------|
| Opening     | Neutral, curious | Enthusiastic greeting |
| Problem     | Calm, reflective | Dramatic, alarmist    |
| Solution    | Firm, logical | "Our solution is amazing" |
| Data        | Technical     | Superlatives         |
| Consequence | Serious, direct | Threatening          |
| Closing     | Slow, deliberate | Rushed goodbye       |

## Pre-recording checklist

- Slides in presentation mode
- Camera and microphone tested
- Quiet environment
- Script on second screen or printed
- Timer visible (target 5 min)
- Script read aloud at least once
- Pauses marked; do not skip them
